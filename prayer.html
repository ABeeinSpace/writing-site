Misunderstanding

Bee 1 day ago

Those who don't know someone are quick to offer thoughts and prayers. As if they care. As if they know and “can relate”. The trouble with humanity is that they often know enough about a topic to think they are right, but not enough to know how truly wrong they are. These people don't want to help you. They couldn’t give less of a crap about you. They just want to show that they are worth something to someone for their own selfishness.

People claim to be concerned about mental health, and pledge to do something different after every shooting. The only thing they do is vilify people who need help and guarantee that they will only get worse and worse until eventually they feel like they have nothing left. And a wild animal backed into a corner will do the only thing it can— fight. Lash out at the next thing it sees, and the next, and the next. Such is the same with a person backed into a corner with nothing to lose.

People who have mental health problems aren’t villains. They aren’t evil people, they just don’t have someone to ground them. They don't have someone to bring them back from the brink of doing something bad to themselves or others. Vilification of these people is not the answer. They don’t want to lash out but they do because they have no other choice. They don’t believe that they are worth anything so they want to get one last jab in before they go.

These people aren’t evil. They simply need someone to let them know that they care. That in their darkest hour, there is someone that they can count on to bring them out of their pit of despair before it's too late. I know nobody will listen to me, I’m just writing this to feel somewhat better. To add my voice to the chorus of teeth-gnashing and screeching but to inevitably be drowned out.

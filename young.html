When We're Young

When we’re young, nothing matters. When we’re young, we’re invincible. When we’re young, we can do anything. We can be an astronaut, a captain of a Spanish galleon, and we can win the Indianapolis 500, all in time for dinner. When we’re young, school is new and exciting. We bounce back up after every injury. Every glance in a different direction is exciting and new and awesome and wondrous. Snow is something that is fun, instead of something that gets all over your car and is something to be cleaned off the driveway. The autumn oranges and reds and the cycle of life is something mysterious to be figured out in the unique ways kids manage to explain away these phenomenon.

And then, the excitement fades. All the beautiful colors and bright wonders of the world, the autumn browns and oranges, falling off into the blinding white of winter and then resurrecting as vibrant greens fade to a dull gray, then to full black and white. Life becomes dull, a chore, something to be suffered through. Treading water, not necessarily living. Trying desperately to survive, to tell the world that you mean something and are something but not necessarily believing what we’re telling the world about our accomplishments. Just going through cosmic motions that something deep inside you tells you to go through even though you don't understand why. Getting stuck in and staying the course because change is terrifying but needing desperately some kind of stimulation to avoid self-hatred.

When we’re young, we’re a firefighter. When we’re young, we’re working for SpaceX on the first manned spaceflight in a Dragon capsule getting blasted to an alien world on a Falcon Heavy. When we’re young, we’re curing cancer and are the leading doctor in mental health, solving crises in schools and burnout among many different professions, all at the same time. When we’re young, we do all of that and more in the same day, and we’re home in time for a good dinner in a familiar house. When we’re young, we have to call our big strong dad to go make sure there’s no monsters under our bed. We go on fantastical adventures with King Arthur and his Knights of the Round Table, slaying dragons and rescuing fair maidens. We’re riding our bikes in a crusade to find the new fad, whether it be Pokémon cards or Beyblades. Every passing moment is a chance for another adventure, capturing a grand castle on a hill overlooking our domain. We’re immortals, having reigned as a peaceful and revered monarch for centuries.

When we’re young, we want nothing more than to see the world, the remote landscapes we see on desktop wallpapers. We can do anything.

Humanity is broken. That much seems to be true. Every night on the evening news, someone’s been shot, someone’s done something racist, someone’s this someone’s that. The negativity can be overwhelming, your belief in your fellow man starting to falter. There’s just shootings, schools putting up gates and walls and fences in the name of reactionary measures for the “just in case worst case” scenario.

This is the exception.

Humanity is not broken. Humanity is not on a downhill spiral. 90% of people don’t care to kill. 90% of people aren’t completely entitled and garbage. 90% of people just want a helping hand and will gladly pay for the person behind them’s meal.

People are kind.

This big bad old world is big. It’s definitely old. What it is not is bad. This world has plenty of good people. It’s just that the “big bad” world is simply misunderstood. The nightly news only highlights the bad stuff simply because it’s NOT the norm. Kindness is common, so a story about someone saving abandoned cats and giving them homes simply won’t sell. However, a story about someone going around and kicking random pregnant cats on the street because they don’t like tabbies will fly off the shelves since it is so abhorrent. People like to be outraged because it isn’t the norm. People like to be outraged because kindness isn't new and exciting. Kindness is old news and that’s a problem.

I know there’s a kindness day but there shouldn’t have to be a day for people to be kind. Kindness day should be every day.
